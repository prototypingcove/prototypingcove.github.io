Overview

Challenge

Seamlessly integrating a compelling narrative into an AR fitness app while maintaining immersion and user engagement.

Opportunity

Crafting a new, unique IP that blended mystery, Hindu mythology, and fitness mechanics and create a story-deep workout experience.

Background

The client, Insane AI, had been looking to gamify various aspects of their AR fitness app, with a particular focus on creating an engaging narrative around their product to increase user retention and drive new users to the app. Their initial goal was to craft a lore-rich world, eventually filling it with stories that players could explore. I was hired as a narrative designer to help them achieve this. I collaborated closely with the production team to develop a pilot worlds, brainstorm narrative concepts and proposed various gameplay ideas, all aimed at helping create a new and unique Intellectual Property from the ground up, based on their specific goals and requirements.

01. Discovery & research

Where to begin?

Though I wasn’t entirely unaware of fitness as an important aspect of life, it was a new genre for me to work with as a narrative designer. I needed to quickly acquaint myself with its cultural nuances, the obstacles people faced, what inspired and drove them, and to learn of the underpinning motifs of the genre as a whole.

At the same time, It was also imperative for me to fully understand the deliverables, requirements, and the product itself.

This was the primary focus of the discovery and research phase.

Understanding the product

The app uses the front camera of the phone to capture a person’s workout routine, which is then analysed by the system’s AI to garner points to the user based on their performance (intensity, speed, and posture).

Arjun’s Story

The pandemic has locked Arjun inside, blurring the days into an endless cycle of work, sleep, and scrolling through screens, with a growing sense of claustrophobia as the walls seem to close in around him. Even stepping outside feels risky, and the repetitive at-home workouts only reinforce how little control he has over his life. On top of that, Arjun is also frustrated with the state of the world, the mishandling of the crisis and the lack of awareness among his peers about the dangers still present.

He craves an escape, something to break the monotony, but at the same time, he wants to feel productive and in control.

What kind of a story would be Relatable for Arjun?

A fitness experience with a narrative that is similarly relatable and that subtly makes him feel powerful, productive and in control of the situation would work well.

It would reflect his own journey of navigating uncertainty, reclaiming control, and proving to himself that he is still moving forward, even when the world isn’t quite there yet.

Competitor Analysis

Competitor Analysis – Zombies run!

Brief

Playing Zombies run!, I found that audio-driven storytelling model to be best suited for the fitness exercise genre, as it allows the player to deeply immerse while disregarding the need for immediate visual indicators (like in normal games).

The in-game diegetic radio station is a great idea. It makes the world feel much larger than it is. And for future reference – A simple in-game radio that the players could listen to in lieu of playing a mission (something that requires less commitment) could work great.

The sudden, periodic zombie chases are quite compelling as well, they create a real sense of urgency and force players into a sprint, tapping into the natural adrenaline rush of being pursued while making high-intensity workouts feel instinctive rather than forced. This mechanic cleverly blends fitness and storytelling, making exercise feel like a gamified survival challenge rather than a routine workout.

Additionally, Zombies, Run! adopts an episodic approach to narrative, which serves as a strong incentive for player retention. A similar structure could work well in a dark techno-punk setting, provided the episodic nature aligns with the intended workout patterns.

Strengths

Audio Immersion: Eliminates reliance on visual interaction, allowing for a seamless fitness experience.

 

Diegetic Storytelling: The in-game radio system keeps players engaged without disrupting their workout.

 

Episodic approach: Regularly released story episodes incentivize frequent workouts.

 

Smaller stories in on large world: having multiple stories within in a larger world make it interesting to explore and incentivize players to explore more.

Challenges

Genre Limitations: Not all players are drawn to a zombie setting, meaning a dark techno-punk theme (if pursued) may face similar audience restrictions.

Potential Interference with Workouts: Some may lack the time or desire to fully engage with gameplay mechanics while exercising.

(It works alright for running, but it may not work very well for calisthenics)

Scaling Narrative Complexity: High-quality audio storytelling requires strong writing, voice talent, and audio production, making scalability a challenge.

(We can only expect that to become a lot more complicated when executing for a multi performance workout.)

Key Take Aways

User Interviews

User Interviews

From interviews with fitness enthusiasts, I gained insight into their motivations, struggles, and the deep personal significance of fitness in their lives. Many saw it as more than just exercise—it was a discipline, a form of self-improvement, or even a means of overcoming adversity. Their perspectives helped shape a narrative that resonated with real-world experiences and emotional connections to fitness.

Industry Research

Industry Research

I looked at how films like Rocky portray personal growth, discipline, and overcoming adversity, seeing how these themes could make fitness feel more meaningful.

TV series like Cobra Kai use character-driven drama to keep audiences invested. A fitness experience could benefit from strong character arcs to deepen engagement.

I explored how audiobooks create engagement without visuals, taking inspiration from how true crime and documentary-style storytelling hold attention through pacing and tone.

Academic Analysis

Academic research

I wanted to learn more about the clinical aspects of how physiology relates to fitness, so I explored various research papers on the topic. In my research, I identified a key theme that I wanted to incorporate into the narrative: the contrast between the physiological need for fitness and modern life.

Humans evolved as endurance hunters, built for sustained physical activity, but modern sedentary lifestyles have created a disconnect, leading to suboptimal health. Research confirms both the long-term and short-term benefits of movement, showing that regular exercise isn’t just beneficial, it’s essential for maintaining overall well-being. Much like an unused car that deteriorates over time, our bodies weaken when inactive, but with consistent movement, they can recover and regain strength.

This insight shaped my approach to designing a narrative that reinforces fitness as a biological imperative rather than just a lifestyle choice.

Developing Pitches

Collecting Themes

In addition to themes suggested by the client, I was encouraged to be “artistically open and define more themes or even better ones.”. Through my research, I was able to collect multiple themes and ideas that I would later incorporate into the different pitches I developed. 

One such idea that would later become the core ethos of the IP was – 

'Death through disuse'

The idea came to me based on the conclusions of a research paper. The idea behind it being that:

  • Humans are endurance hunters by their genetic predisposition, an evolutionary trait developed over the course of large time scales in response to environmental changes.

  • So in an environment of our own making—modern day society characterized by fast, exponential change and sedentary lives—our bodies have struggled to adapt, resulting in suboptimal metabolic functioning and overall health.

  • The paper confirms both long-term and short-term benefits of exercise to the body.

In very simple terms – We are dying faster because we aren’t using our bodies for their intended purpose.

It’s like what happens to a car that hasn’t been used in a very long time—it rusts, its batteries die, and eventually it breaks down. Likewise, modern life allows us the luxury of a sedentary life, which is convenient, but it can also be detrimental to our body. So as with any dusty old car, we need to perform maintenance and to do that we need to supplement our inactivity with exercise and replicate the metabolic state of our hunter/gatherer ancestors.

(Until, of course, we find a way to alter our physiology to suit our current environment, and forever confine ourselves to the dark corners of the virtual dimension.)

The clients liked the idea as it was very relatable for their core set of users, who were either working from home or stuck indoors due to the pandemic. So, the theme not only satisfied the requirements, but it also excited me with a concept that was worth exploring.
